## IV. The Boltzmann Equation at Two Scales

In 1877, Ludwig Boltzmann derived the equation that governs how a bounded intelligence distributes attention
across uncertain alternatives. The single parameter that controls the distribution is temperature.

At low temperature, attention concentrates sharply. Prediction errors are large and informative — when the
model is wrong, the wrongness registers with force, and updating follows. At high temperature, attention spreads
diffusely. Prediction errors are small and uninformative. The mind passes through disconfirming evidence without
catching on it.

The equation applies with equal precision to codon usage and to cognitive inquiry.

In genomic space, the effective temperature of the evolutionary process determines how broadly mutational
pressure explores the codon landscape. At low temperature — strong selection pressure — mutations concentrate
around high-fitness sequences, generating fine-grained updating around the current optimum. At high temperature
— neutral drift — the system explores ker(F) broadly, accumulating synonymous variation that reads as noise
from the outside but represents the exploratory archive from which adaptation will draw when the selection
landscape changes.

In epistemic space, the cognitive temperature of an agent determines how sharply prediction errors register
when claims are falsified. The outlier's temperature in the domain of spice-alcohol neurobiology was low after
2015. The dismissal registered as a genuine prediction error — a disturbance the error-correction mechanism
could not leave unresolved. Low temperature means the wrongness lands hard, and inquiry follows. The peer's
temperature was high. Social confirmation is a high-temperature event: it spreads attention diffusely, returns
a uniformly positive signal, and produces no felt disturbance that would trigger error-correction. The cognitive
landscape, after a social victory, looks flat. No gradient to descend. No inquiry triggered.

The genome solved this problem by routing high-temperature exploration into ker(F) — the space where
exploration is cheap because it does not change the protein. The cost of mutations at ker(F) approaches zero

The outlier solved the same problem through an identical mechanism. The dismissal in 2015 routed inquiry into

## V. Sherman-Morrison and the Two Update Rules

Every perturbation in a connected system — a methylated CpG island, a CRISPR edit at a wobble position, a
social dismissal in a 2015 room — can be expressed as a rank-1 update to the system's information matrix:

```

```

The Sherman-Morrison identity makes this computationally tractable. What would cost O(N³) to recompute from

difference between thermodynamically prohibited and biologically possible. Every cell in your body runs
Sherman-Morrison because it cannot afford the alternative.

But the identity reveals something deeper than a computational shortcut. The rank-1 structure of the update
means that every perturbation to the system affects the system's *response to everything else* — not just the

Now apply this to the 2015 room.
